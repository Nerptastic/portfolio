## Introduction

It all started with a friend’s tattoo power supply breaking down. What seemed like a simple fix turned into a rather comical saga of trial and error. The issue was that the barrel jack had snapped off and the contacts were badly corroded. Little did I know, this seemingly minor repair would lead me on a wild goose chase involving routers, input jacks, and a lot of wasted time and money. Here’s how it all unfolded.

## The Initial Diagnosis

I received the power supply and quickly identified the problem: the barrel jack was not only broken but had corroded contacts. It was clear that a replacement part was needed. But instead of simply replacing the barrel jack, I thought it would be a good idea to salvage one from an old Nighthawk router I had lying around.
Salvaging from the Nighthawk Router

The router was a high-quality piece of hardware, and I figured it would have a durable input jack. I spent a few hours disassembling the router, carefully removing the input jack, and prepping it for the replacement. After reassembling the router and wiring up the salvaged jack to the power supply, I eagerly plugged it in. To my dismay, the jack didn’t fit properly—it was the wrong size.

## The Wrong Size Problem

Frustrated but undeterred, I decided to buy a few new input jacks. I did my research, checked the measurements, and placed an order for what I thought would be the perfect fit. Unfortunately, when they arrived, they turned out to be the wrong size as well. It seemed like every input jack I bought was a little off, and I was getting nowhere fast.

## The Final Solution

Realizing that my approach wasn’t working, I decided to go straight to a reliable source. I ordered the exact input jacks from Mauser Electronics, making sure they matched the specifications of the original part. Once they arrived, I installed the new jack without any issues. The power supply was up and running again, and my friend’s tattoo machine was operational.
Lessons Learned

    Measurement is Key: Always double-check the dimensions and specifications of replacement parts before purchasing. Relying on guesswork or assuming a part will fit can lead to wasted time and money.

    Avoid Salvaging When Possible: Salvaging parts from unrelated devices can be hit or miss. It’s often better to buy the correct part from a reputable supplier, especially when precision is crucial.

    Patience and Persistence: Even though the process was frustrating and costly, it was a learning experience. Sometimes, the journey is just as important as the destination.

## Conclusion

In the end, the repair was successful, and my friend was happy to have their power supply back in working order. While it was a humorous and somewhat costly adventure, I did learn valuable lessons about repair and parts replacement. The whole experience was a reminder that sometimes, a seemingly simple task can turn into a complex project.
